Notes about MPS grading:

By using the Grade Getter feature, you consent to giving over your Infinite Campus student credentials. I won't datamine, or else the cops will get me.

  • This grade calculation utility is somewhat based off of the values from here
    • There's an error in the way that document defines the letter grade ranges.
  • These are the true ranges (in domain/range notation), courtesy of Mr. Millizer:
    • A = [3.405, 4.000]
    • B = [2.745, 3.405)
    • C = [2.145, 2.745)
    • D = [1.595, 2.145)
    • U = [0, 1.595)
  • There is no mathematical way to get a grade of C from just one criterion.
    • AD = A
    • PR = B
    • BA = D
    • MI/O = U
  • At a certain point, you need an unreasonable amount of ADs to get an A.
    • Receiving over 2 zeros, MIs, or BAs across multiple criterions will make it basically impossible to get an A.
  • There's an immense amount of wiggle room to get a grade of A.
  • A zero is infinitely worse than receiving an MI or a BA.
  • Grades are not weighted differently. They are all weighted equally.
  • You can receive zeroes and still have a grade of A (or a B).
  • An AD, AD, AD, and a BA will still equal a course final grade of A.
  • When a criterion grade is combined with a zero (O):
    • AD + O = BA
    • PR + O = BA
    • BA + O = MI
    • MI + O = MI
  • Criterion grades do not translate to simple integers. Instead:
    • AD = 4.00
    • PR = 3.20
    • BA = 2.10
    • MI = 1.10
